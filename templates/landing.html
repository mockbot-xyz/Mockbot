What is a Markov chain?

A Markov chain is a simple statistical model that learns which words tend to follow other words. It reads your chat history and builds a map of word patterns—like "pog" often comes after "that's so". When generating messages, it randomly walks through these patterns to create new sentences that sound like your chat.

This is not AI. It's a basic probability model invented in 1906. No neural networks, no machine learning, just word pattern matching. Think of it like predictive text on your phone, but for whole messages.

What it does

Learns from chat

Analyzes your chat history using Markov chains to map which words commonly follow each other, creating a statistical model of your community's speech patterns.

Posts automatically

Generates new messages by randomly walking through learned word patterns. You control the frequency and message length.

Text-to-speech (optional)

Reads messages aloud using TTS. Requires premium ($2/month) to cover server costs for voice synthesis.

Privacy & Data

Chat Log Retention

Your chat messages are stored on our server. When you add the bot to your channel, it collects and retains your Twitch chat history to build the Markov chain model. This data is used exclusively to generate messages that match your chat's style. We do not share or sell this data.

About Text-to-Speech

TTS does use AI. Unlike the Markov chain bot (which is just statistics), our text-to-speech feature uses Bark by Suno AI, a transformer-based audio generation model. This requires GPU inference, which is why it costs $2/month.

Bark is open-source and runs on our servers. No data is sent to third parties for TTS generation.
